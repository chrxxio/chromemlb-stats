How to read this: Each game lists the probable starting pitchers with a rating from their ERA + WHIP (Tough, Average, Soft), their K%, their BAA (opponents' batting average), and ~IP/st — how deep into games they usually pitch. Below, each team shows the hitters most likely to get a hit off that game's opposing starter, drawn from the 26-man active roster (injured-list players excluded) and ranked by Hit Chance: the odds of at least one hit, combining the batter's AVG with the pitcher's BAA over the at-bats the starter's typical outing length allows (a 6-inning starter gives each hitter more cracks than a 4-inning one). K Chance = strikeout risk vs that same starter (% per PA, projected Ks in parentheses). Colors: green good for the batter / medium / red bad. A HOT flag means batting .300+ over the last 15 days. When no starter is posted yet, both estimates use a league-average pitcher and are marked with *.
Hit Chance formula: per-AB p = (batter AVG × pitcher BAA) ÷ league AVG (~.245); expected AB from IP/start × batters-per-inning ÷ 9 spots; P(≥1 hit) = 1 − (1−p)^AB. Estimates, not guarantees.

Face-Offs is a deep dive on one game: pick a matchup and it breaks down the two teams head to head. You get the wind at the ballpark (MLB's ballpark-relative reading like "10 mph, Out To CF" when posted — usually near game time — otherwise a compass forecast at the stadium), both starters' recent form (last 5 appearances: IP, strikeouts, ERA, WHIP), and for each team's top 5 bats: their last 5 meetings vs this opponent this season, the subset at this ballpark, and their career history vs today's opposing starter — the last times these players actually faced each other.
Analyzes one game at a time (a full-slate version would need hundreds of API calls). Batter-vs-pitcher lines are career numbers and often tiny samples — a 3-for-5 doesn't mean ownership, it means 5 at-bats. Compass wind can't say "blowing out" without park orientation; only MLB's own reading can.

Today's Top Hitter Picks — the best proven bats in the lineups playing on the selected day, ranked by a career résumé score. Choose a pick type: Balanced (AVG 40% + hits 30% + HR 30%), Power (weighted to home runs and HR-per-AB rate), or Contact (weighted to batting average). Each component is measured against MLB benchmarks (.300 AVG, 2000 H, 300 HR, ~.05 HR/AB) so they combine on one scale. A 250+ career at-bat minimum keeps it to established hitters rather than small-sample flukes. Season AVG is shown alongside career AVG so you can see who's in form now versus living on résumé.
This is a career-based ranking, not a matchup play. For situational edge, use the Matchup Targets tab — a top pick facing a vulnerable pitcher is the board's best spot.

Matchup Targets finds the most hittable starting pitchers on the selected day and shows the batters who get to face them. For each game's probable starters it builds a Hittability rating from the vulnerabilities you'd want to attack: a low strikeout rate (can't miss bats) plus high WHIP, hits/9, and HR/9 (gives up a ton of bases). Pick the window — season, last 10, 5, or 3 games — to judge a pitcher on their body of work or their recent form. Pitchers are ranked most hittable first, each paired with the opposing lineup's top bats by season OPS. A strong hitter facing a high-hittability arm is the board's best spot.
Needs probable pitchers posted (usually a day or two out). Pitch velocity isn't in the free API, so "soft-tosser who gives up bases" is captured statistically via low K rate + high hits/WHIP. Pulls game logs for each starter, so it takes a few seconds.
